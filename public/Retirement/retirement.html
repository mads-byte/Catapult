      What is a 401(k)?

      A 401(k) is a retirement savings plan offered by your employer where you contribute part of your paycheck, and that money grows over time 401(k) plans. Think of it as a piggy bank that your future self will thank you for.

      Is It Worth It?:

      • You Choose How Much to Save
      • You Don't Pay Taxes on It... Yet
      • Lowers your taxes today
      • High contribution limits

          What is a Roth IRA?

          A Roth IRA is a retirement account that YOU open yourself (no employer needed), where you pay taxes on the money now, but all withdrawals in retirement are completely tax-free.

          Is It Worth It?:

          • Tax-free withdrawals in retirement
          • No required withdrawals at retirement age, money can stay there as long as you live
          • You Open It Yourself
          • High contribution limits
          3/10

          Key Takeaway for Teens:

          The Power Move:

          If you have a job with a 401(k), contribute enough to get the full employer match (that's free money!). Then, if you can save more, open a Roth IRA and max it out. The combination is unbeatable.

          Why start now?

          A 16-year-old investing $100/month until retirement will have MORE money than someone who starts at 30 investing $300/month. Time is your superpower.

          Life Happens (Age 25–40)

          By now, you'll have a family and with that you will need protection. Enter: Life Insurance.

          Life insurance pays money to your loved ones when you pass away.

          7/10

          Types of Life Insurance

          • Term Life: Cheapest, lasts 10–30 years, no cash value.
          • Whole Life: More expensive, lasts your whole life, builds cash value.
          • Universal Life: Flexible payments, may grow cash value.
          • Variable Life: Investment-linked, higher risk.
          8/10

          Putting It Together

          Now the results of protecting yourself from 18 to Age 67:

          • Delayed both: Lower retirement savings, no insurance coverage.
