Investing

The act of putting your money into something in the hopes that it will grow over time

Why should we invest?

People like to invest for several reasons, but the main reason is to help their money grow over time and build their wealth rather than losing its value due to inflation. Unlike just saving, Investing allows your money to grow through compounding, which means you earn on your original amount and the the returns it generates. This growth can help you reach important goals like buying a house, saving for retirement, or achieving financial independence, while also giving you more freedom and security in the future .

Some good terms to know are:

Investing

refers to buying assets like individual stock, bonds, mutual funds, or exchange-traded funds, with the aim to potentially increasing your wealth over time

Asset

a resource with present or future economic value

Appreciation

the increase in an asset's value over time

Stock

A small piece of a company that you can buy

Stock Market

a system for buying and selling shares of companies

Share

One unit of stock-like owning one slice of the company pie

Dividend

Money a company pays you just for owning its stock

Bond

a loan you give to a company or government - they pay you back with interest

Index Fund

a type of mutual or exchange-traded funds with a portfolio constructed to match or track the components of a financial market index

Mutual Fund

A bunch of different investments bundled together-like a money smoothie

Exchange-rate Funds

funds that are traded on exchanges and usually follow a specific index

Interest

the price paid for the use of borrowed money: money earned for loaning money

Compound Interest

interest earned on both the principal (original) amount and any interest already earned

Roth IRA

an individual retirement account allowing a person to set aside after-tax income up to a specified amount each year. Both earnings on the account and withdrawls after age 59 1/2 are tax-free

IRA

a tax-advantaged retirement savings account to which you contribute either pre- or after-tax money and which grows on either a tax-deferred or tax-free basis

Exchange-traded funds(EFTS)

a type of pooled investment security tjat holds multiple underlying assests, rather than only one

Asset Allocation

an investment strategy that aims to balance risk and reward by apportioning a portfolio'd asset accourding to an investor's goals

Risk Tolerance

investors emotional and psychological capacity to withstand losses in their investment in exchange for potentially higher returns

Security

negotiable financial assets that hold monetarty value and can be traded, such as stock, bonds, options, and mutual fund

Pension

a regular payment made during a person's retirement from an investment fund to which thart person or thier employers has contributed
